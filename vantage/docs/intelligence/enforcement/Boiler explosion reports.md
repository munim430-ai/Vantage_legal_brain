

Multifabs Boiler Blast Incident at Gazipur
## Study Report
On July 3, 2017, Monday evening, a boiler explosion took place at Multifabs Clothing Factory in
Nayapara area of Kashimpur at Gazipur district. In the blast incident, 13 workers were killed and
47 were injured.  Among  the  injured,  there  were also  pedestrians  and  other  garment  workers.
Since  the  incident on  4th  July  '17, fire  service  and  rescue  start rescuing  the  victims and began
searching for the  casualties at the  factory premises.  After rescuing all the  bodies at  10pm  on
the day officially the rescue mission was declared end.
## Incident Details:
Gazipur City Corporation's garment factory, Multifabs Limited situated in Kashimpur, Nayapara
area was to open on July 4, 2017. But on July 3, 2017 Monday the boiler section of the dyeing
unit was started. A number of 80-90 workers were working in the dyeing and finishing section
of  the  ground  floor  of  the  four storied building. In  a tin-shed of  the building there  were two
boilers of 5 and  10  tons  of capacity. Both  the  boilers
were  started in  the  morning before the  incident.  At
7pm,  the 5-ton  boiler  burst  into  a  sudden  blaze.  In
this,  the  lower  floor  of  the  building  of  a  four- storied
building  and  two  side  walls,  doors,  windows  and the
machine  were  flown  away, became  scattered  and
destroyed  in  different  places.  Many  explosions  in  the
factory happened,  walls collapsed, workers stucked
under  broken  structures and  several workers were
injured in the blast.
As a result of the explosion, the buildings of Emma Garments, Islam Group's Unit-2, Tasania and
Mauritius  Garments  and  the  adjoining  factory  buildings  shook  and  windows  were  broken.
Bursting boilers are fragmented at least 500 yards away. The head of a worker falls apart from
the  body  and  found  about  200 yards  away  in the  roof  of  a  building.  The  other  bodies  of  the
deceased   workers   also   fell  on  the   spot.   Immediately   after  the   incident,   the   authorities
concerned disconnected the electricity and gas connections in the area. Locals came forward to
rescue  the victims  and  send  them  to  different  hospitals  and  clinics  including  Shahid  Tajuddin
Ahmed  Medical  College  Hospital,  Konabari,  Kashimpur,  Gazipur and Dhaka  Medical  College
Hospital.  After  receiving information,  fire  fighter of  Joydevpur,  Kaliakoir,  Tongi  and  Savar  EPZ
Fire  Service  started  the  rescue  work.  After  the  incident  in  search  of  the  casualties,  fire  service
personnel started rescue operation on the spot.

On  the  spot,  it  has  been  found  that the  building of  the dyeing unit  and four-storied boiler has
become completely destroyed. The building's pillar, beam, walls, iron pipes, doors and windows
were scattered in pieces. Fire service was searching for removal of debris and the discovery of
missing people. The main garment building was beside the devastating dying unit. All the door-
window glass of the eight-storied garment building was blown up. Another garment factory of
the next faced same condition.
## Casualties:
At the night of the incident, six bodies were recovered from the inside of the factory and 31 of
the  injured  were  admitted  to  local  Shariff  Medical  College  and  16  were  admitted at  Konabari
Clinic. Later, three more of the injured were died. On the next day of the incident, on July 4th,
the  body of  another  man  was found and  another  3  bodies  were  recovered from the  debris  on
Tuesday  morning.  The  body  of  the  dead  was  handed  over  to  the  family.  A  total  of  13  workers
were  killed  and  47  injured  in the  blast  incident.  Two  critically  injured  Rokon  (25)  and  Kamrul
Islam (32) were admitted to Dhaka Medical College Hospital. The rest were admitted to a clinic
in Kanabari, Sharif Medical and Tajuddin Medical College Hospital in Gazipur.

## Investigation Report:
On July 12, the eight-member committee formed by the district administration submitted their
investigation  report  to  the  boiler  explosion  on  the  boiler  blast.  The  next  day  on  July  13,  the
deputy  commissioner  Dewan Muhammad  Humayun  Kabir  officially  published  the  investigation
report in his office. During this time no member of the inquiry committee was present.
The investigation  committee  submitted an  investigation  report  of  28  pages, with  which  91
pages  were  attachments.  During  the  investigation, Boiler's  expert  opinion  and  29  people's
confessions were taken. Deputy Commissioner Dr. Dewan Muhammad Humayun Kabir said that
the factory had 2 boilers of 5 and 10 tons. Of this, the boiler of 5 tons exploded and other boiler
was  also  damaged.  In  the  investigation  report,  the  amount  of  damage  to  the  factory  was
mentioned at Tk 35.62 million.
The report mentions that due to the loss of the pressure gauge of the boiler, due to the delayed
line  of  the  liver,  the  pressure  sensitization  was  created  after  the  liver  shifted  the  boiler  to  the
high  speed  of  the  dead  waist.  Failure  to  release  pressure  by  the  operator  and  the  boiler
maintenance authority and boiler operators due to lack of adequate supervision, the boiler has
exploded.
The report further mentions that the blasted boiler was manufactured by the German company
Omnica  in  1996.  In  1998, it was  established  by Europe Asiatic  Company.  The  rating  was  968
square feet. The boiler was first visited by the inspector of the boiler on June 10, 2003 and got
the registration. The latest chief boiler inspector's office permits to operate 10 times on August
31,  2016  until  June  24, 2017.  It  was  applied  for  renewal  on  June  19.  Even  though  the  boiler
registration period has expired on the day of blasts, it is not possible to say that the lifespan of
the boiler has emerged.
