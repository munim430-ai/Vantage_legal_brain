## Section 1 — Sprint Positioning

### What the BLA 2026 Compliance Sprint IS

- A 3–5 working day compliance guidance and audit-preparation review
- Produces a written Gap Report showing where the factory's documentation stands against Bangladesh Labour Act 2006 requirements
- Produces a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) with prioritized remediation steps
- Produces draft or updated versions of up to 5 specific policy/procedure documents
- Delivered in PDF and editable DOCX format, in both Bangla and English
- Includes one 60-minute review call within 7 days of delivery

### What the BLA 2026 Compliance Sprint IS NOT

- Not an official audit conducted by BSCI, WRAP, SA8000, Sedex, or any third-party audit body
- Not legal advice or a legal opinion
- Not a certification of compliance
- Not a guarantee of a passing audit result
- Not representation of the factory at any audit, inspection, or labour court proceeding

### Approved wording for all Sprint communications

| Use | Do not use |
|---|---|
| compliance guidance | legal advice |
| audit-preparation support | certified compliant |
| gap report | official audit result |
| corrective action plan | guarantee audit pass |
| documentation readiness | verified compliant |
| remediation support | certification |
| audit-preparation review | verified by VANTAGE |
| compliance posture | passed audit |

### Deliverable boundaries

The Sprint covers:
- Documentation review against BLA 2006 and relevant audit framework checklists
- Written gap report and corrective action plan
- Up to 5 policy/procedure documents drafted or redrafted
- One 60-minute review call

The Sprint does **not** cover:
- Ongoing monthly monitoring (that is the Retainer)
- Implementation beyond the 5 policy drafts
- Physical visits outside Dhaka/Gazipur (transport charged separately if required)
- Representation at any audit
- More than 5 policy documents (additional documents are billed under Document Factory rates)

## Section 4 — Document Request List

Send this request to the factory on Day 0 via WhatsApp. Group by category for clarity. Emphasize: documents are reviewed confidentially under the signed NDA and MSA; VANTAGE will not share them.

Do not demand any document the factory is unwilling to share. Record "not provided" and note it in the gap report.

### Group A — Employment Documents

- Appointment letters (sample: 3–5 workers, different grades)
- Age verification / NID or birth certificate records at hiring (sample)
- Service register or worker register (all current workers)
- Employee personal file sample (1–2 files)

### Group B — Wage Records

- Payroll sheet (most recent month)
- Wage slip sample (3–5 workers)
- Payment bank/bKash confirmation records (most recent month)
- Any deduction records with authorization

### Group C — Working Hours and Overtime Records

- Daily attendance register (most recent week)
- Overtime register or overtime consent forms (most recent month)
- Weekly rest day register or policy

### Group D — Leave and Maternity Records

- Leave register (annual, casual, sick leave for last 12 months)
- Maternity leave register and benefit payment records (last 24 months if applicable)
- Maternity policy document (if exists)

### Group E — Grievance Records

- Grievance policy or procedure document
- Grievance register or log (last 12 months)
- Any resolved grievance records (anonymized acceptable)

### Group F — Harassment and Disciplinary Records

- Anti-harassment policy document
- Harassment complaint procedure
- Disciplinary procedure document
- Disciplinary action records (last 12 months, anonymized)

### Group G — Safety and Fire Records

- Fire drill records (last 12 months)
- Fire safety policy or evacuation procedure
- Safety committee meeting minutes (last 12 months, if applicable)
- Any RSC/Accord remediation records (if factory volunteers)

### Group H — Welfare and Worker Committee Records

- Participation committee election/selection records
- Participation committee meeting minutes (last 12 months)
- Workers' rights notice or trade union rights notice

### Group I — Policy Documents

- Employment contract template (if exists)
- Factory rules/code of conduct
- Compliance or social responsibility policy (if any)
- Training records (compliance, fire safety, BLA training)
- CAP tracker or previous CAP record (if factory volunteers)

### Group J — Buyer / Audit Communications (voluntary only)

Do not request these. Accept only if the factory volunteers them.

- Previous audit reports (buyer or third-party)
- Previous CAP letters from audit body
- Buyer social compliance questionnaire responses

## Section 8 — CAP Structure

Use this table structure for the Corrective Action Plan. One row per gap item. Sort by risk level (Critical first, then High, Medium, Low).

| Gap ID | Theme | Risk Level | Issue Description | Recommended Action | Responsible Person | Target Date | Evidence Required | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| GAP-01 | | Critical | | | HR Manager | [date] | | Open |
| GAP-02 | | High | | | | [date] | | Open |

**Gap ID format:** `GAP-[NN]` (sequential per Sprint)

**Issue Description rules:**
- Describe as a documentation observation, not a legal determination
- Approved: "Wage slips were not available for review during the assessment period"
- Forbidden: "Factory is in violation of BLA 2006 Section X"

**Recommended Action rules:**
- Describe as remediation support, not compliance mandate
- Approved: "Draft and implement a monthly wage slip template meeting BLA 2006 Section 123 requirements"
- Forbidden: "You must comply with BLA 2006 or face penalties"

**Status values:** Open / In Progress / Resolved (factory updates this during implementation)

## Section 14 — Risk and Legal Boundary

Munim must be able to state these clearly at any point during Sprint delivery. If a client asks a boundary question, use the exact wording below.

| If a client asks… | Say exactly… |
|---|---|
| "Does this mean we are compliant?" | "This review identifies documentation gaps and recommends corrective actions. It does not determine compliance or certification status." |
| "Will we pass our audit?" | "VANTAGE provides audit-preparation support. Audit outcomes are determined solely by the relevant audit body — BSCI, WRAP, Sedex, or whoever is conducting your audit. We cannot make any representation about audit outcomes." |
| "Can you certify our documents?" | "VANTAGE is not a certification body. We cannot certify documents or issue compliance certificates." |
| "Is this legal advice?" | "No. This is compliance guidance and audit-preparation support. VANTAGE is not a law firm. If you need legal advice, please consult a licensed advocate." |
| "Can you represent us if there is an audit?" | "No. VANTAGE provides documentation preparation and guidance support. We cannot represent factories at audits, government inspections, or labour court proceedings." |

**Written statement (include in every delivery email):**

> "Keystone Consultancy trading as VANTAGE provides compliance guidance and audit-preparation support. VANTAGE is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. VANTAGE is not affiliated with any audit body and does not guarantee audit outcomes. All deliverables are compliance guidance documents only."

## Section 15 — Stop Conditions

Stop Sprint delivery immediately if any of the following conditions arise. Do not complete or send the delivery package.

| Condition | Stop condition code | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Client asks Munim to create false, backdated, or fabricated records | `FAKE_DOC` | Stop immediately. Say: "VANTAGE cannot create false or backdated records. This would expose both VANTAGE and the factory to serious legal risk. I cannot continue under these conditions." |
| Client asks to backdate any document | `BACKDATE` | Same as above. |
| Client asks to hide worker complaints or grievance records | `HIDE_GRIEVANCE` | Stop. Say: "VANTAGE cannot help suppress or hide worker grievance records. This conflicts with our service terms and legal positioning." |
| Client asks to misrepresent compliance status to a buyer or audit body | `MISREP` | Stop. Say: "VANTAGE provides accurate compliance guidance only. We cannot make representations about compliance status on behalf of a factory." |
| Active government investigation, DIFE inspection, or labour court matter discovered | `GOVT_INVEST` | Pause Sprint. Do not deliver documents. Say: "An active investigation changes the situation — VANTAGE needs to pause while you consult a licensed advocate. I'll hold all files until you confirm it is safe to proceed." |
| Possible child labour discovered (any worker under 18 on hazardous work) | `CHILD_LABOUR` | Stop commercial delivery. Address safety issue first. Do not use this as a paid deliverable finding — it is a safety escalation. |
| Serious imminent safety issue discovered (structural, fire, electricity) | `SAFETY_RISK` | Same as above. |
| Client requests VANTAGE to claim affiliation with BGMEA, BSCI, or any audit body in the deliverables | `FALSE_AFFIL` | Refuse the specific request. Proceed with all other deliverables only if the client drops the request. |

**Escalation:** Record the stop condition in the CRM `sprint_sales_pipeline` → `sprint_status` = Paused / Closed Lost. Add a note under `notes`. Do not commit any client data to the repo.
