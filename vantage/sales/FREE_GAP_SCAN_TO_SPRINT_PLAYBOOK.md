## Part 3 — How to Ask the 25 Questions

### General rules

- Read the question exactly as written. Do not paraphrase — the question wording is legally positioned.
- Accept "yes" or "no" as the primary answer. Do not accept "usually," "mostly," or "we try to" as a yes.
- If the answer is ambiguous, mark "no" and note it in the evidence column.
- For Critical and High questions answered "no," ask: *"Can I see the document briefly, just so I can note it?"* Do not demand. Accept refusal and mark evidence as "not produced."
- Do not share the risk level or points for each question during the scan. Scoring comes at the end.
- Q25 is the only open-answer question. Write the exact document names the factory cannot produce.

### How to handle "not sure" answers

> "If you are not certain whether the document exists or is complete, that is a 'no' for now. If you find it later and want to send it to me, I can update the notes."

Do not accept "we have it somewhere" as a yes. Mark it no.

### How to handle resistance to a question

> "This question is important because this is one of the first things an auditor checks. You do not have to show me the document right now — I am just noting whether it exists."

If they become defensive or refuse to continue, pause. Say:

> "There is no obligation. I am here to help you understand where the risk is. If you would prefer to stop here, I can prepare a partial summary from the answers so far."

Never pressure. A partial scan that leads to a follow-up is better than a forced session that loses the relationship.

### Question-by-question prompts

Use these follow-up prompts only when the initial yes/no answer is unclear. Do not use these as the primary question — always ask the question from the bank first.

| Q# | If the answer is unclear, ask: |
|---|---|
| Q1 | "If I asked to see one worker's personnel file right now, would there be a signed appointment letter in it?" |
| Q2 | "Is there a checklist or form completed at the time of hiring that records the worker's NID or birth certificate?" |
| Q3 | "Is there one register — physical or digital — where all current workers are listed with their join date and grade?" |
| Q4 | "If I compared today's attendance sheet to last month's wage sheet, would the hours match?" |
| Q5 | "Are normal hours and overtime hours written separately in any record?" |
| Q6 | "Is there a form a worker signs if they agree to work overtime, or a notice that goes up before overtime is scheduled?" |
| Q7 | "For each worker, is their weekly rest day recorded somewhere?" |
| Q8 | "Does each worker receive a wage slip or written statement showing their pay each month?" |
| Q9 | "Does the payroll sheet show the worker's grade, basic pay, overtime rate, and any deductions separately?" |
| Q10 | "If a deduction is made, is there a record showing why and whether the worker was informed?" |
| Q11 | "Is there a register for annual leave, sick leave, and casual leave — with applications and approvals?" |
| Q12 | "For any worker who has taken maternity leave in the past two years, is there a record of the leave and the maternity benefit payment?" |
| Q13 | "Is there a written policy — posted or distributed — that tells workers how to raise a complaint without fear?" |
| Q14 | "If a worker raised a grievance six months ago, is there a record of what happened and how it was resolved?" |

## Part 4 — How to Handle Missing Evidence

When a factory cannot produce a document during the session, follow this sequence.

**Step 1 — Acknowledge without alarm:**

> "That is fine. I am noting that this document was not available during our session. This does not mean it does not exist — it means we could not verify it today."

**Step 2 — Score it as a gap:**
Mark the question as "No" and "Evidence not produced." This feeds the risk score.

**Step 3 — Note it for Q25:**
If the missing document is one the factory names in Q25, it becomes a "double gap" — a Critical question with no evidence available on demand. Record the document name exactly.

**Step 4 — Do not push:**
Do not ask more than once per question. If they say they will find it later, note: "Follow up after session — document pending."

**Step 5 — Double gaps go in the report immediately:**
Any Q25 document that also maps to a Critical question (Q1, Q2, Q9, Q12, Q13, Q15, Q16, Q25) becomes the first item in the corrective action plan.

## Part 5 — How to Calculate the Risk Band

Do this immediately after the last question, before presenting results.

### Step 1 — Fill the scoring worksheet

Use the template from `RISK_SCORING_MODEL.md §8`. For each "No" answer, enter the weight. For Q25, apply the sliding rule:

| Q25 answer | Points |
|---|---|
| 0 documents named | 0 |
| 1 document named | 3 |
| 2 documents named | 5 |
| 3 or more documents named | 8 |

### Step 2 — Sum the Risk Score

Add all points. The maximum is 141.

### Step 3 — Calculate the Compliance Score (show to factory)

```
Compliance Score = 100 − round((Risk Score ÷ 141) × 100)
```

Do not show the raw Risk Score to the factory. Show only the Compliance Score.

### Step 4 — Assign the Risk Band

| Risk Score | Band |
|---|---|
| 0–15 | Low Risk |
| 16–35 | Medium Risk |
| 36–60 | High Risk |
| 61 or above | Critical Risk |

### Step 5 — Check Sprint Triggers

Tick each trigger that applies:

| Trigger | Check |
|---|---|
| Q25 names 3 documents | [ ] |
| Q12 AND Q13 AND Q15 all "No" | [ ] |
| Q15 AND Q16 both "No" | [ ] |
| Q8 AND Q9 both "No" | [ ] |
| Q2 = "No" | [ ] |
| Q24 = "No" AND any Critical question = "No" | [ ] |
| Risk Score ≥ 61 | [ ] |

Any single trigger means the Sprint is the recommended offer.

## Part 9 — Objection Handling

### "BDT 55,000 is too expensive."

**Do not negotiate. Say:**

> "I understand. The Sprint fee covers a full gap report, corrective action plan, and up to five remediated policy documents — all delivered in 3 to 5 working days, in both Bangla and English. The alternative is receiving those findings from an auditor after a CAP is issued. A CAP suspension costs far more than BDT 55,000 in lost orders alone.
>
> If the full Sprint is not the right fit today, I can start with specific documents — for example, the grievance procedure or the maternity policy — at BDT 3,000 to 5,000 per document. But the Sprint package is the most efficient path."

**Never go below BDT 55,000 on the Sprint.** If the factory cannot proceed, offer the Document Factory (individual documents at BDT 3,000–5,000 each) or the Retainer.

---

### "We need time to think / discuss internally."

**Say:**

> "Absolutely. Before you go, can we agree on a time to reconnect? I would like to follow up in 48 hours — Wednesday [specific day and time]. If your audit window is [X weeks away], we need to start the Sprint no later than [date] to deliver before the audit."

Set a specific date and time before leaving. Do not say "I will call you." Get a commitment to a window.

For High and Critical risk bands: follow up in 48 hours maximum.
For Medium risk: follow up within 5 days.

---

### "We already have someone handling this."

**Say:**

> "That is good to hear. Do you know what their scope covers? The reason I ask is that the BLA 2026 amendments and the 2023 minimum wage revisions have created some specific documentation requirements that not all consultants are tracking yet. I am happy to do a quick document review alongside your existing support — sometimes a second pair of eyes finds things that were missed."

Do not criticize the existing consultant. Position VANTAGE as a specialist layer, not a replacement.

---

### "We will wait until after the audit."

**Say:**

> "That is one option. The risk is that if the auditor identifies Critical gaps during the audit, they may issue a major CAP — and some buyers suspend shipments until the CAP is closed. At that point, you would be remediating under time pressure with a shipment on hold. The Sprint costs BDT 55,000 before the audit. A suspended order can cost several times that. I am not saying this to pressure you — I am saying it because I have seen it happen."

If they still decline, leave a copy of the gap report and the quotation. Note the factory for follow-up after the audit result is known.

---

### "Can you guarantee we will pass the audit?"

**Say — clearly and firmly:**

> "No. VANTAGE does not guarantee audit outcomes and we never will. Audit results are determined solely by the audit body — BSCI, WRAP, Sedex, or whoever is running your audit. What the Sprint does is improve your documentation posture and reduce the visible gaps that are most likely to be raised as nonconformities. That is what we commit to — thorough, structured preparation. Not a guarantee."

This is a legal boundary. Never suggest a pass outcome is guaranteed, even informally.

---

## Part 12 — Stop Conditions

Stop the scan or do not proceed to the commercial workflow if any of these conditions appear.

| Condition | Action |
|---|---|
| Factory is under active government investigation (DIFE, Labour Court) | Stop. Do not advise. Tell them: "This situation requires a licensed advocate. VANTAGE cannot advise during an active investigation." |
| Lead explicitly asks for legal representation or legal opinion | Stop commercial pitch. Say: "VANTAGE provides compliance guidance, not legal services. For legal advice, please consult a licensed advocate." |
| Lead demands a guaranteed audit pass as a condition of engagement | Do not proceed. Say: "VANTAGE does not guarantee audit outcomes. That is not a service we offer and we cannot make that commitment." |
| Lead asks VANTAGE to certify compliance or issue a certificate | Stop. Say: "VANTAGE is not a certification body. We cannot issue compliance certificates. The relevant audit body does that." |
| Lead requires VANTAGE to claim affiliation with BGMEA, BSCI, Sedex, or any audit body | Stop. Say: "VANTAGE is an independent compliance advisory service. We are not affiliated with any of those bodies and we cannot claim to be." |
| Lead requires buyer-logo use in VANTAGE materials without written buyer consent | Decline that specific request. Proceed with everything else only if the factory drops the requirement. |
| Any question answer suggests imminent zero-tolerance worker safety issue (e.g., possible child labour on site) | Stop the commercial pitch. Address the safety issue directly first. Ask: "Are any workers currently under 18 on site?" If yes, advise immediate removal from hazardous work and consultation with a licensed advocate before any commercial engagement proceeds. |

Escalate any stop condition to `vantage-legal-risk-guard` before proceeding further.
